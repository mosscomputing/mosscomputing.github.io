About MOSS Computing

MOSS (Message-Origin Signing System) gives autonomous agents cryptographic identities. Every output signed at the source. Verifiable without centralized authorities. Post-quantum secure. Local-first, zero-trust execution.

SDK live. Integrations shipping for CrewAI, AutoGen, LangGraph, LangChain.

Started coding at 14 to modify video games. 30 years since — embedded systems, real-time graphics, physics simulation, security protocols.

Engineering lead at Electronic Arts on Need for Speed. Global Head of Technology at Nexon through IPO.

Holds two patents in cryptographic authentication — ultrasonic identity verification (US 10050723) and proximity-based spatial authentication (US 20180241577) — both solving the same problem MOSS addresses: binding identity to action without centralized trust.

Longer-horizon: SEEP, a quantum authentication protocol using continuous-variable entanglement. Hardware-bound signatures. Physically unclonable. No stored keys. Paper in preparation.

Agents will outnumber humans. Identity infrastructure must exist before that happens.
